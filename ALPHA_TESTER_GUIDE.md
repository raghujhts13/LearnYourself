# LYS (OpenMAIC) – Alpha Tester Guide

Welcome to the alpha testing phase! Thank you for helping us shape the future of **LYS (OpenMAIC)**, our new AI-assisted learning platform. 

Imagine you are an educator or a trainer with a head full of ideas or a folder full of old documents, and you need to create an engaging, interactive course quickly. LYS is designed to be your intelligent co-pilot.

Here is a plain-English breakdown of every single functionality you’ll be testing:

### 1. The "Classroom" Organization System
* **Your Digital Hub:** Instead of scattered files, everything you create is organized into virtual "Classrooms." Think of this as your master folder for a specific subject or group of students.
* **Persistent Data:** Your classrooms are saved securely on the server. You can close your browser, switch devices, or go into incognito mode, and your lessons will still be there waiting for you. Point to note is that the classrooms are saved in indexDB and therefore if you clear your browser data, you will lose your classrooms. Any data generated inside chrome can only be viewed using chrome and not firefox or edge.

### 2. The Content Ingestion Engine (Bring Your Own Materials)
* **Read Everything:** You don't have to start from scratch. You can upload PDFs, Word Documents (.docx), plain text files (.txt), or your old PowerPoint presentations (.ppt/.pptx). 
* **The AI Reader:** Once uploaded, our system reads these documents and automatically understands the core material so it can help you teach it.

### 3. The Two Magic "Generation Modes"
* **"AI Generate" Mode:** Tell the platform a topic, or point it to a document you uploaded, and the AI will build an *entire, cohesive lesson from scratch*. It will break down the topic logically, write the script, and create the visual scenes for you.
* **"Use My Slides" Mode:** Have an existing PowerPoint that you like? Import it directly. The platform won't overwrite your work; instead, it will parse your existing slides and upgrade them into our interactive format.
* **Live Web Research:** If your lesson needs up-to-date, real-world context, the AI has "Web Search Integration." It connects to the actual internet to inject real-time facts into the lesson it builds for you.

### 4. Automated Assessments & Quizzes
* **Smart Interruption:** With a simple toggle switch, you can ask the AI to naturally build quizzes right into the flow of your course. It evaluates what it just taught and creates interactive questions designed to check the student’s understanding on the spot.

### 5. The Interactive "Stage" (Presentation Canvas)
Once your lesson is generated, you manage it on what we call the "Stage." This isn't just a static slideshow.
* **Dynamic Whiteboard:** You can draw on the slides during playback, stamp shapes, and insert live, data-driven charts. 
* **Advanced Content:** Are you teaching math or science? You can insert complex math formulas natively.
* **Rich Media:** You can seamlessly embed and play videos right inside the canvas.
* **Presenter Tools:** To guide the viewer’s eye, the Stage includes tools like a "Spotlight" (darkening the screen except for one area) and a digital "Laser Pointer."

### 6. Automated Narration (The AI Voice Actor)
* **Text-to-Speech Voiceovers:** You do not need to stand in a sound booth and record yourself. The platform reads the script on each slide and generates incredibly realistic, customizable voiceovers for your scenes. It essentially acts as the narrator for your curriculum.

### 7. Multi-Language Accessibility
* **Translations:** The platform supports language translation capabilities, allowing education to transcend language barriers. This will be an ongoing feature we want you to test for accessibility.

### 8. Easy Exports
* **Take It With You:** Even though LYS is an advanced platform, we don't hold your content hostage. After you use the AI to build your dream lesson, you can export it outwards as a traditional PowerPoint (`.pptx`) file to send to colleagues or play strictly offline.

### 9. Flexible Customization (The Settings)
* **Swap the AI "Brains":** Behind the scenes, we use massive AI models (like ChatGPT, Claude, etc.). Through a simple user interface, you can tell the platform which AI mind you'd prefer to use for writing, which service you'd like to use for the narrator's voice, and even which search engine to use for web-research—all without needing a degree in computer science.

---

### Tester Goals

* **Play and Break:** Try out the generation tools with different documents. 
* **Interact:** Play around with the whiteboard tools, spotlight, and laser pointer during playback.
* **Listen:** Test out the automated narration to see how well the AI explains the material.
* **Organize:** See if creating and organizing classes within classrooms feels natural and intuitive.

Have fun, and please let us know your honest thoughts and any bugs you encounter!
